How To Deliver This Without Memorizing It

The only things you need to remember:

Four punchlines. That's it. Everything else is you talking naturally around them.

PO — "The status deck wrote itself."

Architect — "Root cause analysis becomes graph traversal."

Developer — "The context found her."

Director — "Not a status deck. Institutional proof."

Four pain openers — shorter each time. PO gets the full Friday story (20 sec). Architect gets a question + two lines. Developer gets the Rajesh joke. Director gets two sharp sentences. By the fourth persona, the audience already knows the pattern — they're waiting for the solution, not the pain. Give it to them fast.

One repeating phrase. "Same graph. Different lens." Say it at least twice. It's the one sentence the audience will remember after lunch.

Practical tips:

Use the inline outlines. Each persona section has a numbered roadmap right at the top. If you lose your place, look at the roadmap — it tells you what step you're on and what's next.

Talk to the room, not the screen. Click the thing. Let it load. Then turn to the audience. The screen is evidence. Your voice is the story.

When someone asks "how" — defer. Say: "That's exactly what I'm going to show you in the next section." This demo is about what the system does for each role. The architecture deep-dive answers how it works.

Silence is your weapon. After the Delivery Graph renders — wait. After a punchline — wait. Two seconds of silence makes the line land harder than thirty seconds of explanation.

The audience hooks are optional but powerful. The ADR question, the Rajesh laugh, the status-deck show of hands — these turn a demo into a conversation. Use whichever ones the room energy supports. Drop them if you're running tight on time.

Transitions build momentum. Each handoff teases the next persona: "All those plans Joshi locked? Someone has to guard the architecture..." — this keeps the audience leaning forward instead of resetting between sections.
